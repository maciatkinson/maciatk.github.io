Dopamine and Notifications

Many social media platforms use notifications to keep users engaged. Every time someone receives a like, comment, or message, the brain releases dopamine. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter that is connected to reward and motivation.

These small rewards make people want to check their phones repeatedly. Because the rewards are unpredictable, users often check social media frequently throughout the day.

This pattern is similar to other reward systems that encourage repeated behavior.
